Exotic Plant Care

Exotic plants can add a unique touch to your home. Here are some tips on how to care for them so they thrive and brighten up your space!

General Tips

Lighting

Exotic plants require good lighting. Place them in areas with bright, indirect sunlight. Some plants can tolerate partial shade, but most need good sunlight to grow well.

Watering

Avoid overwatering your plants! Most exotic plants do not like standing water. Water them when the top layer of soil feels dry. It’s important not to let them become waterlogged.

Temperature

Exotic plants thrive in warm conditions. Keep them within a temperature range of 18°C-25°C (64°F-77°F). Avoid sudden temperature fluctuations, as they can harm the plant.

Fertilizing

Fertilize your plants regularly, especially during the growing season. Use fertilizers designed for exotic plants and follow the instructions on the packaging for proper dosing.

Humidity

Many exotic plants prefer higher humidity levels. Place them on a tray with water and pebbles, or use a humidifier to maintain optimal humidity for your plants.

Soil Type

Exotic plants generally prefer well-draining soil. Use a mix designed for tropical or exotic plants to ensure good drainage and prevent root rot. Adding perlite or sand can also help improve soil aeration and drainage.
